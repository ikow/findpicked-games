How to Play Go

Objective

Control more territory (empty intersections) than your opponent by surrounding areas with your stones.

Basic Rules

  • Black plays first; players alternate placing one stone per turn on an empty intersection.
  • Stones do not move once placed.
  • Liberties: A stone's liberties are the empty intersections directly adjacent (up, down, left, right). A group shares liberties.
  • Capture: When a stone or group has zero liberties, it is captured and removed from the board.
  • Ko rule: You cannot immediately recapture a single stone that was just captured (to prevent infinite loops).
  • No suicide: You cannot place a stone where it would have zero liberties, unless it captures enemy stones.

Ending the Game

  • Pass your turn if you have no useful moves. When both players pass in succession, the game ends.
  • Scoring uses Chinese rules (area scoring): count your stones on the board plus the empty intersections you surround.
  • White receives 6.5 points of komi (compensation for going second).

Tips for Beginners

  • Try to surround large areas rather than chasing individual stones.
  • Keep your groups connected — isolated stones are easier to capture.
  • Play near the center and third line from the edge for a balance of territory and influence.
