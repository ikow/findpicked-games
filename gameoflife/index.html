Conway's Game of Life is a cellular automaton with four simple rules:

  • Any live cell with 2 or 3 live neighbours survives
  • Any dead cell with exactly 3 live neighbours becomes alive
  • Every other live cell dies (loneliness or overcrowding)
  • Every other dead cell stays dead

How to play:

  • Click or tap cells to toggle them alive/dead
  • Drag across the grid to paint multiple cells at once
  • Press Play to run the simulation, Step to advance one generation
  • Use the Speed slider to adjust generations per second
  • Load a pattern to try a famous configuration like the glider or Gosper gun
  • The edges wrap — patterns that fly off one side return from the other
  • Your highest surviving generation count is saved to the leaderboard
Invented by John Conway, 1970.
